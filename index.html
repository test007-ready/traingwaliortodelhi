Why the Gwalior-Delhi Rail Route Defines Luxury Travel in India

The 313-kilometer rail journey from Gwalior Junction (GWL) to New Delhi (NDLS) is not merely a transit; it's a curated passage through history, culture, and architectural splendor. This route is served by over 70 daily and weekly trains, including some of Indian Railways' most prestigious services. The distance is covered in as little as 3 hours 31 minutes on the Vande Bharat Express, while the leisurely Taj Express takes about 5 hours, allowing you to soak in the changing landscape from the Chambal ravines to the Yamuna plains.
